---
title: "Trusted devices"
description: "Review and revoke devices that can sign in without an extra verification step."
---

When you sign in from a new device, Flotick may ask you to verify with a 6-digit code sent to your email. You can **trust** that device so future sign-ins skip the extra step.

[IMAGE: Trusted devices list with revoke button per row]

## Where to find this

Profile → Security → **Trusted devices**.

## What's listed

For each trusted device, Flotick shows:

- A short device name (browser and OS)
- The IP and rough location of the trust action
- The last time the device successfully signed in

## Revoke a device

Click **Revoke** on any row to remove the trust. Next sign-in from that device will require email verification again. Revoke any device you don't recognize immediately.

## Revoke everything (sign out all sessions)

Profile → Security → **Sign out all sessions** revokes every refresh token. You stay signed in only on the device making the request. Use this if you suspect compromise.

## What about 2FA?

Trusted devices are separate from two-factor authentication. With 2FA on, an untrusted device requires:

- Your password
- A 6-digit TOTP code

A trusted device may skip an additional email-code prompt for new device verification, but the TOTP step still applies.

## Tips

- Trust only the devices you regularly use.
- Review the list every few months and prune devices you no longer use.
- If you've been on a public computer, **don't** trust the device — Flotick won't ask, but be deliberate.
