---
title: "Sprint planning"
description: "Run a sharp planning session that ends with a committed sprint."
---

A good sprint planning session takes 60–90 minutes and yields a sprint everyone believes in. Here's a flow that works inside Flotick.

## Before the meeting (lead, 15 minutes)

<Steps>
  <Step title="Trim the backlog">
    In the project's Tasks page, sort the backlog by priority. Re-prioritize, close stale tickets, and verify ownership.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Estimate unestimated stories">
    Story points are fast estimates. Aim for 1, 2, 3, 5, 8 — keep it consistent across sprints.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Check capacity">
    Look at the team's recent velocity in [Team Analytics](/features/team-analytics). Plan for ~80% of the trailing average.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create the sprint">
    From the project, click **New sprint**. Set a clear sprint goal, dates, and capacity in points.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## In the meeting (team, 60–90 minutes)

<Steps>
  <Step title="State the goal">
    Read the sprint goal aloud. Discuss whether it's the right outcome for this cycle.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Drag in candidate tasks">
    From the project backlog, move tasks into the sprint. Group by theme or epic where helpful.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Walk each task">
    For each task, confirm:

    - Owner (assignee, reviewer, tester)
    - Acceptance criteria
    - Estimated points and any dependencies
  </Step>

  <Step title="Commit">
    Sum the points. If you're above the planned capacity, drop something. When the team agrees, click **Start sprint**.

  </Step>
</Steps>

## During the sprint

- Discuss scope events in standups when they happen — every change is logged
- Use the **AI Sprint summary** template at the midpoint for a sanity check
- Watch the burndown chart; if it diverges sharply from ideal, it's a signal, not a verdict

## End of sprint

<Steps>
  <Step title="Complete the sprint">
    Click **Complete sprint** in the sprint detail page. Velocity is calculated automatically.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Run the retrospective">
    Capture **What went well**, **What can improve**, **Action items**, and a 1–5 **Sprint rating** directly in Flotick. The retrospective is preserved with the sprint forever.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Decide the fate of unfinished tasks">
    Move them to the next sprint or back to the backlog with a clear note.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Tips

- **Don't oversize sprints.** Two-week sprints with consistent length are easier to compare than mixed lengths.
- **Resist scope creep.** When new urgent work arrives, drop something equivalent rather than adding net new.
- **Treat the retrospective as a real artifact.** The team's memory degrades; the retro doesn't.
