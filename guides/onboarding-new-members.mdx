---

A consistent onboarding gets new members to their first task quickly. Here's a playbook to run when someone new joins.

## Before they accept the invite

<Steps>
  <Step title="Pick the right role">
    Most new joiners are **Members**. Promote to **Manager** later as scope expands. Keep **Admin** to the founder/owner — only one is allowed per organization.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Decide their department">
    Set a **Department** and **Sub-department** so reporting and analytics route correctly.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Pre-assign a starter project">
    Identify a Public project they can join immediately (or add them as a project member to a Private one).
  </Step>

  <Step title="Send the invite">
    From **Members**, click **Invite member**. Pick role and add a clear note in the invite (optional).
  </Step>
</Steps>

## When they accept

The invitee follows [Accept an invitation](/get-started/accept-invitation) and runs through onboarding. They land on the dashboard.

## Day 1 (1 hour total)

- Walk them through the [Dashboard tour](/get-started/dashboard-tour)
- Show them how to **check in / out** and start a [Focus Session](/features/focus-sessions)
- Open the team's **Tasks** page; assign a small starter task
- Add them to relevant projects (Private projects need explicit ProjectMember add)
- Show how to find files in the **Files** browser and what tags you use

## Week 1

- Pair them on a sprint with a senior member (set both as task collaborators where helpful)
- Have them read the relevant **Help** pages from the in-app **Help** section
- Connect their **Telegram** for important notifications

## Pre-payroll setup (Admin)

If your org runs payroll in Flotick:

- Set their **compensation** with annual CTC, monthly gross, and basic salary
- Add bank, PAN, and UAN
- Pick the right **salary structure**

The first run that includes them will pro-rate based on join date.

## Tips

- Keep starter tasks small but real. The first **Done** is psychologically important.
- Tell new members where to ask questions — usually `@mention` a buddy in comments.
- Audit pending invites monthly; revoke ones that won't be used to free seats.
