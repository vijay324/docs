---
title: "Daily routine"
description: "A 5-minute daily checklist to make Flotick a habit."
---

A short, consistent rhythm keeps your team's data accurate and your Flotick experience clean. Here's a recommended daily routine.

## Morning (about 3 minutes)

<Steps>
  <Step title="Check in">
    Open the **Check in** dialog. Pick your work type (Office, Remote, Hybrid). The live work timer starts.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Open the Personal Dashboard">
    Look at:

    - **Upcoming deadlines** — anything due today?
    - **My tasks** — what's `In Progress` or `Review`?
  </Step>

  <Step title="Open the Personal Board">
    Drag the day's first task to `In Progress` and start working.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## During the day

- Move tasks across columns as their state changes — keep your status truthful
- Reply to comments and `@mentions` from your **Inbox**
- Use **Log work** on a task to record hours as you go

## Before lunch (about 1 minute)

- Glance at your team's [Personal Board](/features/personal-board) or the project board to unblock teammates
- Acknowledge or respond to `@mentions`

## End of day (about 2 minutes)

<Steps>
  <Step title="Update tasks one last time">
    Move anything you finished to `Done`. Anything not finished, add a brief note in the comments so tomorrow's you doesn't lose context.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Check out">
    Open **Check out**. Add a short work description; click **Enhance with AI** if you want a quick polish.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Acknowledge AI Insights">
    Open the **AI Insights** tab in your Inbox. Dismiss what's irrelevant; act on what's not.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Friday (extra 2 minutes)

- Generate the **Weekly summary** AI template and share with the team
- Capture a couple of bullets in your sprint comments for the retrospective
- Skim the [Team Analytics](/features/team-analytics) for trends

## Tips

- The lighter the routine, the more likely it sticks. Don't over-engineer.
- A check-in/check-out habit pays off enormously in the monthly payroll cycle and in honest team metrics.
