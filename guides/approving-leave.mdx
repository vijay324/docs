---
title: "Approving leave"
description: "How Managers and Admins review and decide leave requests in Flotick."
---

Approving leave in Flotick is a one-click flow with full audit and automatic attendance integration.

## Where pending requests show up

- The **Inbox** sends you a notification
- The **Leave → Team requests** tab lists every pending request


## Make the decision

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the request">
    Click the row to expand or open the detail. You see:

    - Member, leave type, dates, half-day flag
    - Days requested
    - Available balance for the type
    - Reason
  </Step>

  <Step title="Decide">
    Click **Approve** or **Reject**. You can leave a short review comment either way.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What happens on approval

- The request status flips to `Approved`
- For each leave date, an attendance row is created or updated with status `Leave`
- The member's leave balance for that type is decremented; a transaction is logged
- The member is notified

## What happens on rejection

- The request status flips to `Rejected`
- The balance is released
- The member is notified with your review comment

## Edge cases

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Member doesn't have enough balance">
    The behavior depends on the **Leave policy**. With `requiresBalance` on, balance shortfalls are blocked at submission. Without it, you can still approve — the balance goes negative or the deficit is treated as Loss of Pay per policy.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Overlapping requests">
    Whether overlapping requests are allowed is a policy setting. If allowed, both can be approved; if not, the second is blocked at submission.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Cancelling an approved request">
    Cancellation rules are policy-driven. Many orgs require a manager to reject the approved request and create a new one if dates change.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Auditing

Every balance change writes a `LeaveTransaction` row. Admins can review the full history per member from the **Leave** page audit drawer.

## Tips

- Decide quickly — pending leave is a stress factor for the requester.
- Add a short review comment on rejections; "blackout window" or "load-balancing" goes a long way.
- Pair this guide with the [Leave](/features/leave) feature page for a member-facing overview.
