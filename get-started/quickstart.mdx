This guide walks a brand-new user through creating an organization, inviting their team, and shipping their first task.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create your organization">
    Visit [app.flotick.com/auth/signup](https://app.flotick.com/auth/signup) and sign up with email or Google.

    Pick an organization name. Flotick generates a URL-safe slug like `acme-co` that becomes your workspace URL: `app.flotick.com/acme-co/...`. You become the **Admin** of the new organization automatically.

    [IMAGE: Organization signup form with name and slug fields]
  </Step>

  <Step title="Complete the onboarding wizard">
    After sign-up you land on the onboarding wizard. Complete:

    - **Profile** â€” first name, last name, job title, profile photo
    - **Organization details** â€” work mode (Office, Remote, Hybrid) and standard work hours
    - **Invite teammates** (optional) â€” kick off your team's onboarding now or later

    You can skip optional steps and return to them through your profile.

    [IMAGE: Multi-step onboarding wizard showing the profile step]
  </Step>

  <Step title="Invite your team">
    From the sidebar, open **Members** and click **Invite member**.

    Enter their email and pick a role:

    - **Admin** â€” one per organization, full access
    - **Manager** â€” manage projects, sprints, and tasks; approve leave
    - **Member** â€” work on their own tasks, request leave, log work

    Invitees receive a tokenized email link valid for 7 days.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create your first project">
    Open **Projects** and click **New project**.

    Fill in:

    - **Name** and short description
    - **Visibility** â€” Public (every member can see it) or Private (only assigned project members)
    - **Project lead** â€” owner accountable for delivery
    - **Target date** (optional) â€” drives the project health calculation

    Once created, the project shows up in the list with a `Health` indicator that updates as work progresses.

    [IMAGE: Project creation dialog with name, description, lead, and visibility fields]
  </Step>

  <Step title="Plan your first sprint">
    Inside the project, open the **Sprints** tab and create a new sprint.

    Provide a goal, start and end dates, and a planned capacity in story points. The sprint starts in the `Planning` state. Drag tasks from the backlog into the sprint, then click **Start sprint** when you're ready.

    Only one sprint per project can be active at a time.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create and assign a task">
    Open the **Tasks** page or the project board, then click **New task**.

    Enter a title, pick a type (`Story`, `Bug`, `Task`, `Epic`, or `Spike`), priority, story points, and assignee. Optional fields include reviewer, tester, labels, and due date.

    The task appears in the assignee's **Personal Board** instantly via real-time updates.

    [IMAGE: New task dialog with title, type, priority, assignee fields]
  </Step>

  <Step title="Move work across the board">
    Drag the task across columns: `Backlog â†’ Todo â†’ In Progress â†’ Review â†’ Testing â†’ Done`. Each transition is validated against your organization's workflow rules.

    Reaching `Done` marks the task complete and contributes to sprint velocity.

    [IMAGE: Kanban board with task cards across status columns]
  </Step>

  <Step title="Check in for the day">
    Open the **Attendance** dialog from the sidebar or top bar to check in. Pick your work type â€” Office, Remote, or Hybrid â€” and start the live work timer.

    At the end of the day, check out and add a brief work description. Use **Enhance with AI** to polish the prose if you like.
  </Step>
</Steps>
