<Steps>
  <Step title="Create or join an organization">
    [Sign up](/get-started/sign-up), or open the invitation link sent by an Admin or Manager.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Complete your profile">
    Add your name, role details, department, work type, and mobile number during [onboarding](/get-started/onboarding).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create a Project">
    Open **Projects**, click **New project**, then set its name, dates, visibility, lead, and members.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create work">
    Open **Tasks** and click **Create task**. Choose whether it is an individual Task or part of a Sprint workflow.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Plan a Sprint">
    Create a Sprint inside the Project, set its dates and capacity, then assign Sprint Tasks.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start the day">
    Open **Inbox** for your relevant Tasks. Use **Attendance** to check in and check out.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
Press <kbd>Ctrl</kbd> + <kbd>K</kbd> or <kbd>⌘</kbd> + <kbd>K</kbd> at any time to find a page or action.
</Tip>

## A practical first-day checklist

After the first setup, make sure the organization can actually use the workspace:

1. Invite at least one teammate and confirm they can accept the invitation.
2. Add that member to the first Project.
3. Create a Task with an assignee, priority, and due date.
4. Add a comment and attachment so the team sees the collaborative workflow.
5. Create a short Sprint when the Project will run in iterations.
6. Ask every member to check their Inbox and complete their Profile.
7. If your team uses time operations, configure attendance and leave policy before the first requests arrive.

The initial configuration does not need to be perfect. Begin with the default workflow, then customize it after the team has real work to evaluate.
