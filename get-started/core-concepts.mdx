## Work hierarchy

| Concept | Purpose |
| --- | --- |
| **Organization** | Contains your members, policies, work, files, and subscription. |
| **Project** | Groups a body of work, its team, Sprints, Tasks, files, and environments. |
| **Sprint** | A time-boxed cycle within one Project. |
| **Task** | A deliverable with ownership, status, priority, dates, and collaboration. |

Projects, Sprints, and Tasks receive readable IDs such as `OPS-P-001`, `OPS-S-004`, and `OPS-T-037`. You can open detail pages with these IDs.

## Task ownership

A Task can identify four responsibilities:

- **Assignee:** completes the work.
- **Reviewer:** reviews the result.
- **Tester:** validates it.
- **Reporter:** created or requested it.

Individual Tasks use `To Do`, `In Progress`, and `Completed`. Sprint Tasks use the organization workflow, which can include `Backlog`, `Sprint Backlog`, `Todo`, `In Progress`, `Review`, `Testing`, `Blocked`, and `Done`.

## Roles

| Role | Typical access |
| --- | --- |
| **Admin** | Organization, billing, policy, member, and work administration. |
| **Manager** | Team and delivery management within the role's permission ceiling. |
| **Member** | Assigned and accessible work, personal attendance, and leave. |

On paid plans, per-member overrides can narrow or grant allowed operations without changing a member's role. See [Member permissions](/admin/member-permissions).

## Organization routes

Signed-in pages include your organization slug: `app.flotick.com/<org-slug>/tasks`. This keeps navigation and access scoped to the correct organization.

## How access is evaluated

Flotick evaluates access in layers. First, you must be an active member of the organization. Next, the app checks your role and any per-member permission override. Finally, it checks Project visibility and membership for Project-scoped work. This means being able to see the Tasks page does not automatically grant access to every Task or Project in the organization.

## Status, estimates, and reporting

Status describes where work is in a workflow. Priority communicates urgency. Story points estimate relative effort for Sprint planning; logged hours record time already spent. Keeping these fields current gives Sprints, Analytics, and Orbit reliable information to work from.

## What belongs where

Use a Project for an ongoing delivery area, a Sprint for a bounded iteration, a Task for a deliverable, a subtask for a short checklist item, a comment for discussion, a file for an artifact, and a task link for a relationship to another Task. This separation keeps views and reporting useful as the organization grows.
