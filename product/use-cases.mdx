## Engineering teams

**Primary goals**
- Improve sprint predictability.
- Reduce context-switching between board, tasks, and reporting.
- Keep project health visible in real time.

**Recommended setup**
1. Configure custom workflow statuses in `/{orgSlug}/settings/workflow`.
2. Run sprint cadence from `/{orgSlug}/sprints`.
3. Track active delivery in `/{orgSlug}/board` and `/{orgSlug}/tasks`.
4. Monitor velocity and burndown in `/{orgSlug}/analytics`.

## Operations and people teams

**Primary goals**
- Centralize attendance, leave, and compensation.
- Create policy consistency without manual enforcement overhead.

**Recommended setup**
1. Configure attendance and leave policies in `/{orgSlug}/settings/policies`.
2. Roll out check-in/out from `/{orgSlug}/attendance`.
3. Manage leave request flow in `/{orgSlug}/leave`.
4. Operate salary structures and runs in `/{orgSlug}/payroll`.

## Multi-department organizations

**Primary goals**
- Keep one system of record across product, engineering, and HR operations.
- Align team-level execution with leadership reporting.

**Recommended setup**
1. Segment ownership by roles (`Admin`, `Manager`, `Employee`).
2. Use org-wide dashboards for team status and blockers.
3. Add AI summaries for leadership reporting cadence.
4. Use platform-admin for portfolio-wide visibility when running multiple orgs.

## SaaS operators and platform owners

**Primary goals**
- Operate subscriptions, monitor infrastructure, and maintain compliance.

**Recommended setup**
1. Use `/super-admin/organizations`, `/super-admin/users`, `/super-admin/subscriptions`.
2. Review `/super-admin/infrastructure` and `/super-admin/audit`.
3. Enforce 2FA and IP controls for privileged operations.

<Note>
  Flotick supports both product-delivery and workforce operations in one architecture, eliminating cross-tool reconciliation work.
</Note>
