---

## What it does

Sprints group tasks into time-boxed cycles with a shared goal and measurable progress.

## Why it matters

- Improves delivery predictability.
- Helps teams commit to realistic scope.
- Makes progress visible through velocity and burndown trends.

## How to use it effectively

1. Define a sprint goal before selecting tasks.
2. Add only tasks that are ready to execute.
3. Track progress daily on board and sprint views.
4. Review completed vs planned work at sprint close.

## Sprint success checklist

- Goal is clear and outcome-based.
- Scope matches team capacity.
- Blockers are surfaced within 24 hours.
