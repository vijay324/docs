A Sprint belongs to one Project and includes a goal, objectives, dates, capacity, visibility, members, Tasks, files, comments, and delivery metrics.

## Lifecycle

`Planning → Active → Review → Retrospective → Completed`

Authorized members advance a Sprint through its lifecycle. Starting an active Sprint begins its scope ledger and delivery tracking.

## Create and plan a Sprint

<Steps>
  <Step title="Set the frame">
    Choose a Project, title, goal, objectives, success criteria, start and end dates, and story-point capacity.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set access">
    Choose visibility, add Sprint members, and attach planning files.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add work">
    Create Sprint Tasks or move eligible Project backlog Tasks into the Sprint.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start">
    Review the commitment, then move the Sprint from `Planning` to `Active`.
  </Step>
</Steps>

You can use [AI-assisted drafting](/features/ai-drafting) to prepare editable Sprint content.

## Sprint workspace

| Tab | What it contains |
| --- | --- |
| Overview | Health, work board, carried work, and unassigned Tasks |
| Insights | Commitment Coach, Auto Standup, and the active-Sprint scope ledger |
| Members | Sprint membership and capacity context |
| Files | Sprint attachments |
| Comments | Sprint discussion |
| Analytics | Burndown and Sprint delivery metrics |

**Commitment Coach** compares committed points with capacity and highlights risk. **Auto Standup** prepares a daily summary from current Sprint activity; Admins and Managers can regenerate it. The **Scope ledger** records additions, removals, estimate changes, completion, and reopening while the Sprint is active.

## Complete and carry work

When unfinished Tasks remain, choose selected or all work and move it to another eligible Sprint or back to the Project backlog. Carried Tasks retain their source Sprint.

A Sprint supports one retrospective with what went well, what should improve, action items, and a rating. Keep it with the Sprint so the outcome is available during later planning.

<Note>
Velocity is based on story points completed in `Done`. Scope changes remain visible instead of being folded into the original commitment.
</Note>
