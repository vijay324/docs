Shift management is available to Admins and Managers.

## Shift definition

A shift includes a name, unique code, type, working days, start and end times, break duration, and working-hour rules. Types include fixed, flexible, rotational, night, and split.

Configure:

- Grace period for late arrival
- Half-day and full-day hour thresholds
- Overtime threshold
- Active status
- Default status

Flotick supports overnight shifts where the end time falls on the following day.

## Assign a shift

Choose a member, an active shift, an effective-from date, optional effective-to date, and notes. A new overlapping assignment automatically ends the member's previous active assignment.

Use **All Shifts** to manage definitions and **My Assignments** to review effective-dated assignments available to you. A shift with active assignments cannot be deleted until those assignments are ended.

## Create a shift

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open All Shifts">
    Click **New Shift**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set schedule and type">
    Enter a name and unique code, choose the shift type, working days, start time, and end time. Flotick detects a cross-midnight schedule from the times.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set attendance thresholds">
    Configure working hours, break duration, grace minutes, half-day hours, full-day hours, and overtime-after hours.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Activate or set default">
    Keep the shift active to make it assignable. Mark one as default when it should be the organization's standard option.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Manage assignments

Assignments are effective-dated. Use an end date for a temporary schedule or omit it for an ongoing one. When a new assignment overlaps an active assignment, Flotick automatically closes the older assignment to avoid two active shift rules for the same day.

Review **My Assignments** to confirm the schedule associated with your member record. If it is wrong, ask an Admin or Manager to correct the assignment rather than changing an attendance entry manually.
