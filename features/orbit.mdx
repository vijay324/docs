**Orbit** is Flotick's conversational, workspace-aware AI assistant. Ask it anything about your organization's work — *"How is the Mobile sprint tracking?"*, *"What's overdue for Priya?"*, *"Which projects are at risk this week?"* — and Orbit reads your live, org-scoped data and answers in clear narrative, with every number backed by real records.

Orbit is the front door to all of Flotick's AI. From the same surface you can chat, run one-click reports, and hand off bigger changes to the [Orbit Agent](/features/ai-agent).

**Where to find it:** **Inbox → Orbit** (`/<org-slug>/inbox/orbit`). Access is gated to **Admins and Managers**.

## What you can ask

Orbit is built for **workspace questions** — anything grounded in your projects, sprints, tasks, members, attendance, and leave.

- **Status** — *"How is the Q3 Platform sprint doing?"*, *"Are we on track to finish by Friday?"*
- **People** — *"What is Aryan working on?"*, *"Who has the most open tasks right now?"*
- **Risk** — *"What's overdue?"*, *"Which tasks are blocked and for how long?"*
- **Comparison** — *"Compare this sprint's velocity to the last one."*
- **Workload** — *"Is anyone overloaded this week?"*

<Note>
  Orbit answers questions about **your workspace**. Off-topic asks (general trivia, coding help, world knowledge) are politely declined — Orbit stays focused on your organization's data and never sends it anywhere it doesn't belong.
</Note>

## How Orbit answers accurately

Orbit is engineered so the numbers in an answer are **always real**, not invented by the model.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Understands the question">
    A lightweight classifier figures out what you're really asking and which people or projects you named — so *"how is Aryan doing?"* is treated as a real question, not rejected as off-topic.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fetches the right data">
    Orbit builds a compact, **org-scoped** snapshot of exactly the records your question needs. If you name a person or project, their full open-task list is pulled in directly, with timezone-correct due-date and overdue math.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Writes the answer">
    Google Gemini narrates the snapshot under a locked instruction set that demands grounding, accurate counting, and concise answers.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verifies every number">
    Before you see it, a numeric verifier checks that every count in the answer exists in the underlying data. On a mismatch, Orbit retries once with authoritative totals; if it still can't confirm, it falls back to a plain factual summary rather than risk a wrong number.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Answers stream in with a typewriter effect, so you start reading immediately.

## Quick Reports

The **Quick Reports** panel gives you the most-asked questions as one-click buttons — no typing required. Reports cover sprint health, workload distribution, deadline risk, and team activity. Some are deterministic (computed straight from your data) and some are AI-narrated; either way they're scoped to your organization.

## Make Orbit speak your language (Harness)

Every organization has its own vocabulary. The **Harness** lets Admins and Managers teach Orbit your terminology and the names it should recognize, so answers match how your team actually talks.

- **Terminology and known names** — register the projects, squads, and aliases your team uses.
- **Learns from feedback** — when you 👎 an answer, Orbit periodically reviews recent negative feedback and drafts prompt improvements.
- **Approval-gated** — suggested improvements stay **pending** until an Admin approves them. Nothing changes how Orbit answers until a human signs off, so the assistant never silently drifts.

Open the **Harness** settings from the Orbit page to edit terminology, review pending suggestions, and approve or reject them.

## Privacy and isolation

- Every query is filtered to **your organization only** — data is never returned across tenants.
- Conversations are private to the person who created them; reopening or deleting is per-user.
- The exact data snapshot behind each answer is stored for audit but **never shown to the client** and never leaves your org boundary.
- If AI is temporarily unavailable, Orbit degrades gracefully with a friendly message, and deterministic Quick Reports still work.

## Tips

- Name people and projects explicitly — Orbit recognizes them and pulls their full task lists.
- Ask follow-ups in the same thread; Orbit remembers the recent turns.
- Use 👍/👎 on answers — your feedback feeds the Harness so Orbit gets sharper for your team.
- For questions and analysis, use Orbit **Chat**; to actually *make changes*, hand off to the [Orbit Agent](/features/ai-agent).
