---
title: "Orbit Chat"
description: "Ask grounded questions about your organization's delivery and workforce data."
---

Open Orbit from the floating launcher or `/<org-slug>/inbox/orbit`. Chat is available to every organization member; answers are scoped to data the member can access.

## Ask a question

Use natural language for questions such as:

- What is at risk this week?
- Which Tasks are overdue?
- Summarize the active Sprint.
- How is work distributed across the team?
- What changed since the previous reporting period?

Orbit focuses on Flotick organization data. Responses can include structured claims and supporting workspace context. You can provide positive or negative feedback and regenerate an answer.

## Reports and history

Report templates are grouped into leadership, delivery, and people categories. Conversations are saved to history. You can reopen or delete a conversation and pin up to five items in the current browser.

Free organizations use automatic model selection. Pro and Max members can select supported Orbit modes. Every answer consumes the plan's current AI or Orbit allowance shown in settings.

<Note>
Orbit can summarize and explain data, but you should verify important delivery or people decisions against the source records it references.
</Note>

## Use report templates

Open the report area in Orbit to choose a template. Templates are organized into leadership, delivery, and people categories so a manager can quickly find a Sprint summary, deadline-risk assessment, workload balance, team-health brief, or executive digest. The exact templates visible can depend on available data and plan entitlement.

When you run a template, Orbit builds a response from current organization data and adds it to the conversation. Treat the template as a starting point for review; open the cited Task, Project, Sprint, attendance, or leave surface when you need to take action.

## Manage conversations

Start a new conversation for a distinct question. History preserves prior chats and Agent sessions. You can reopen a conversation, delete it from history, or pin up to five conversations in the current browser. Pinning is a local convenience—it does not pin the item for other members.

## Choose a model

Free-plan use is automatically selected by Flotick. Eligible Pro and Max users can choose **Auto**, **Orbit**, or **Orbit Pro** before asking a question. The interface shows the credit cost of the selected mode. If usage is exhausted, wait for the next allowance period or ask the organization Admin to review the plan.

## Get better answers

Name a Project, Sprint, member, status, or date range when it matters. Ask one decision-oriented question at a time, such as “Which active Tasks are overdue in Project Atlas?” rather than “What is happening?” This gives Orbit less ambiguity and makes the result easier to verify.
