---
title: "Trash"
description: "Restore or permanently remove deleted Tasks, Sprints, Projects, members, and files."
---

Flotick keeps supported deleted items recoverable for 30 days. Trash is organized into tabs for Tasks, Sprints, Projects, members, and files.

Each row shows who deleted the item, when it was deleted, and how many days remain. Authorized members can:

- Restore one item to its original location
- Select and restore multiple items
- Permanently delete one or more items

<Warning>
Permanent deletion cannot be undone. Items also become unavailable after their recovery window expires.
</Warning>

Restoring a member uses a plan seat. Restoring a Project can be blocked by plan limits or by a missing parent relationship. Restore parent items before their children when necessary.

## Restore an item

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the correct tab">
    Choose Tasks, Sprints, Projects, Members, or Files.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review expiry and context">
    Check the deletion date, remaining days, associated Project, and who deleted the item.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Restore">
    Click **Restore** for one item, or select multiple rows and use the bulk restore action. Confirm when prompted.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Restoration returns the record and its retained related data to normal access where the original parent and permission conditions still exist. For example, restoring a Task also restores its standard Task context; restoring a file returns it to its supported association.

## Permanently delete

Use permanent deletion only when the record is no longer needed and recovery should be impossible. The confirmation is deliberate because this action cannot be reversed. Items that are not permanently deleted remain eligible for automated cleanup after their 30-day recovery period.

## Access notes

Your role determines which Trash tabs and actions you see. Admins have the broadest recovery controls, particularly for members. If an expected item is not visible, confirm you are in the right organization and ask an Admin to check whether it was already permanently deleted or has expired.
