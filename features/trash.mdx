title: "Trash"
description: "30-day soft-delete recovery for tasks, sprints, projects, and members."
---

Flotick uses **soft delete** for major entities. Deleted items move to **Trash** at `/<org-slug>/trash` and stay recoverable for **30 days**. After that, they're permanently removed by an automated background job.

[IMAGE: Trash page with tabs for Tasks, Sprints, Projects, Members]

## What can be deleted (and recovered)

- **Tasks** — including comments, work logs, history, and links
- **Sprints** — including their task associations
- **Projects** — including child sprints and tasks
- **Members** — soft-deleted accounts

Each row in Trash shows the original item, who deleted it, and how many days remain before permanent deletion.

## Restore an item

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Trash">
    From the sidebar, click **Trash**. Use the entity tabs to switch between Tasks, Sprints, Projects, and Members.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Find the item">
    Search by name or filter by deletion date.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Restore">
    Click **Restore**. The item flips back to active state and reappears in normal lists.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Cascade behavior

When you delete a parent (e.g., a project), Flotick cascades the soft delete to all children using the same expiry timestamp. Restoring the parent **does not** automatically restore children deleted before the parent — only those deleted as part of the cascade.

This is intentional: you don't want to revive earlier independent deletions when you restore a project.

## Permanent delete (Admin)

Admins can permanently delete an item before the 30-day window with the **Delete permanently** action. Use this for sensitive data you want gone immediately.

<Warning>
Permanent deletion is irreversible. The item, its history, and its children cannot be restored.
</Warning>

## Auto-cleanup

A daily background job runs around 02:00 UTC and hard-deletes items whose 30-day window has expired. You don't need to manage retention manually.

## Tips

- Use **Trash** as a safety net, not as a workflow. If you regularly use deletes-then-restores, consider archiving or moving to a different status instead.
- Members in Trash retain their email — it's only freed up after permanent deletion. If you need to re-invite someone, restore first.
