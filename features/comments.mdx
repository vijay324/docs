Flotick comments turn tasks and sprints into a living thread of context. Comments support threading, `@mentions`, likes, and Markdown.

[IMAGE: Threaded comments on a task with replies and likes]

## Where you can comment

- **Tasks** — full threaded comments inside the task detail dialog
- **Sprints** — sprint-scoped comments for goal discussion and retro notes

## Compose a comment

The comment composer accepts:

- **Markdown** including bold, italic, code, links, and lists
- **GitHub-flavored Markdown** features like task lists and tables
- **Mentions** with `@` to autocomplete teammates
- **Code fences** for snippets

Posting is **rate limited** (about 30 per hour per user) to deter spam and accidental loops.

## Reply and thread

Click **Reply** under any comment to nest a reply. Threads keep the conversation grouped by topic.

## Like a comment

Click the heart icon to like a comment. Likes are visible to the author and other readers — useful for low-noise acknowledgements.

## Edit and delete

You can edit your own comments at any time. The comment shows an `edited` indicator after the first edit. Admins can delete any comment in their organization. Deleted comments leave a small placeholder so the thread structure remains readable.

## Mentions and notifications

`@mention` triggers a notification to the mentioned member, including in their [Inbox](/features/inbox), and through Telegram if they've connected it.

## Encryption

If your organization enables comment encryption, the body is stored encrypted (AES-256-GCM). The `@mentions` and metadata stay queryable for routing notifications.

## Tips

- Use **threads** to keep multi-thread discussions navigable.
- Lead with a one-line summary; put detail in fenced code or collapsibles.
- Replace one-word "ok" comments with a 👍 like to reduce noise.
