## Task fields

| Area | Available fields |
| --- | --- |
| Classification | `Epic`, `Story`, `Task`, `Bug`, or `Spike`; `Critical`, `High`, `Medium`, or `Low` priority |
| Planning | Project, optional Sprint, story points, estimated hours, due date, and labels |
| Detail | Description, considerations, attachments, subtasks, comments, and work logs |
| Access | `Public` or `Private` visibility |
| Responsibility | Assignee, reporter, reviewer, tester, and supported collaborator roles |

A non-Epic Task can have one parent Epic in the same Project. The Epic shows child progress; deeper nesting is not supported.

## Individual and Sprint Tasks

An **individual Task** has no Sprint and uses `To Do → In Progress → Completed`. It does not use an assignee, reviewer, tester, or acceptance criteria. Recurring schedules are the exception: their generated individual Tasks can carry the configured assignee.

A **Sprint Task** belongs to a Sprint, requires an assignee, and uses the organization's Sprint workflow. The default stages are `Backlog`, `Sprint Backlog`, `To Do`, `In Progress`, `Review`, `Testing`, `Blocked`, and `Done`.

Private Tasks are limited to their participants and privileged roles.

## Find and update work

The Tasks page supports **List**, **Board**, **Calendar**, and single-Project **Backlog** views. Switch between all, assigned, created, and team scopes when your permissions allow it.

Search and filter by status, priority, type, Project, Sprint, responsibility, label, and date. Select multiple rows to change status, priority, or Sprint, or move them to Trash. Backlog view also supports ranking work within a Project.

<Note>
Tasks in completed Sprints are hidden by default. Enable **Show completed sprint tasks** when you need them.
</Note>

## Create a Task

<Steps>
  <Step title="Choose the Project">
    Start from Inbox, Tasks, a Project, or a Sprint. Every Task belongs to a Project.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose individual or Sprint work">
    Leave Sprint empty for individual work, or choose a Sprint to enable Sprint responsibility and workflow fields.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add planning detail">
    Set type, priority, estimate, due date, labels, visibility, and an optional parent Epic.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add execution context">
    Add description, acceptance criteria where supported, considerations, files, subtasks, and comments.
  </Step>
</Steps>

You can use [AI-assisted drafting](/features/ai-drafting) to prepare editable Task content.

## Status, time, and history

Move a Task from its status control or by dragging it on a board. Flotick validates the destination against the applicable workflow.

Use **Log work** to record hours and an optional description. Task history records status and responsibility changes. Due dates power Calendar, overdue filters, Analytics, and Orbit risk questions; they do not prevent a status change.

Deleting a Task moves it to [Trash](/features/trash). Create, edit, assign, and delete actions depend on role, permissions, visibility, and Project access.
