Focus Sessions are short, timed deep-work blocks. Optionally tied to a task, they auto-create a work log when you finish, closing the loop between time and tasks.

[IMAGE: Focus session widget with running countdown timer]

## Start a session

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the focus widget">
    The widget lives in the sidebar or top bar. Click **Start focus**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Pick a task (optional)">
    Search for one of your assigned tasks. The session can run without a task too.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set the planned duration">
    Common defaults are 25, 45, or 60 minutes. The countdown starts immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Stop or cancel

When you're done, click **Stop**. If you got interrupted, **Cancel** discards the session.

What happens on stop:

- The session is saved with `status = completed`
- If a task was attached and the session ran for at least a minute, Flotick **auto-creates a work log** on that task
- The task's `Time spent` is updated automatically

Cancelled sessions never create a work log.

## See your history

The widget surfaces the most recent sessions. Aggregate focus minutes also show on your [Personal Dashboard](/features/personal-dashboard) and feed the analytics rollups.

## One active session at a time

You can only have one active session at any moment. Starting a new session cancels the previous one.

## Tips

- Pair a focus session with `In Progress` on a task — your time and your status agree.
- For long deep-work blocks, start a 60-minute session, take a short break, then start another. The auto-log keeps your task time accurate.
- Use the focus widget alongside [Personal Board](/features/personal-board) for a tight planning-execution loop.
