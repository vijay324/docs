---
title: "Departments"
description: "Group members into a two-level hierarchy for reporting and routing."
---

Departments give your organization a structural map. Members belong to a **Department** and optionally a **Sub-department** under it.

[IMAGE: Departments page with parent and child structure]

## Why use departments

- Cleaner **analytics** — slice productivity, attendance, and leave by department
- **Reporting** — payroll exports and audit logs show department context
- **Routing** — some notification rules and approval flows can use department membership

## Create a department

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Settings → Departments">
    The page shows existing departments and their sub-departments.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add a department">
    Enter a name (e.g., `Engineering`, `Operations`, `Sales`). Names must be unique within the org.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add sub-departments (optional)">
    Under any department, add sub-departments (e.g., `Engineering → Platform`, `Engineering → Mobile`).
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Assign a member

Open the member's profile or detail page. Pick a department, then optionally a sub-department under it. Sub-department selection is constrained to the chosen parent.

## Move or rename

You can rename a department or sub-department at any time. Moving a sub-department under a different parent reassigns it; existing member assignments retain the new parent.

## Delete

Deleting a department reassigns its members to "no department" by default. Be deliberate — some analytics views key off department membership.

## Tips

- Keep your hierarchy two levels deep. Going deeper rarely earns its complexity in analytics.
- Use department names matching how the team actually talks. Avoid jargon-only names.
- Departments can be a quick proxy for org-wide policies — for example, different leave behavior by department, when policy supports it.
