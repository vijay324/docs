---
title: "Member permissions"
description: "Fine-tune what individual members can do — on top of their role — without changing roles."
---

<Info>Available on **Pro** and **Max**. On Free, members get standard role permissions only.</Info>

Roles (`Admin > Manager > Member`) set the baseline. **Member permissions** let you grant or restrict specific capabilities for one person — for example, give a Member read access to Analytics, or stop a Manager from deleting tasks — without promoting or demoting them.

**Where to find it:** **Members → open a member → Permissions tab** (`/<org-slug>/members/<username>`).

## How it works

- A permission is a **module + operation** pair, e.g. `analytics.view`, `tasks.delete`.
- Operations are `view`, `create`, `edit`, `delete`.
- Overrides only move within the **role ceiling** — you can never grant a capability the role isn't allowed to have.
- Roles never change; overrides simply fine-tune within them.

## Modules you can tune

`inbox`, `tasks`, `sprints`, `projects`, `board`, `attendance`, `leave`, `analytics`, `files`, `env storage`, `members`, `holidays`, `shifts`, `payroll`.

Organization settings stay role-only (Admin) and aren't override targets.

## Set an override

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the member">
    Go to **Members**, open the person, and select the **Permissions** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Toggle capabilities">
    Flip individual `view / create / edit / delete` toggles per module. Greyed toggles are above the role ceiling and can't be enabled.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Changes apply almost immediately — the member doesn't need to sign out. Per-module **Reset** returns them to role defaults.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Who can edit whom

| Editor | Can edit |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Admin** | Any member (not themselves or other Admins). |
| **Manager** | Member-role people only, and can't grant a capability they lack. |
| **Member** | No access. |

Admins always have full access and can't be restricted.

## Effects

- The member's **sidebar** hides modules they can't view.
- Restricted actions are hidden or blocked in the UI and rejected by the API.
- Overrides are preserved if you downgrade to Free (ignored while on Free), and re-activate on upgrade.

## Tips

- Start from the role and grant the *minimum* extra each person genuinely needs.
- Use a Member + `analytics.view` override instead of promoting someone to Manager just for dashboards.
- Reset to role defaults whenever an override stops being necessary — it keeps access easy to reason about.
