---
title: "Member permissions"
description: "Adjust a member's module operations without changing their role."
---

Per-member overrides are available on Pro and Max. On Free, each role uses its default permissions.

Open a member and choose **Permissions**. Each supported module exposes one or more operations: `View`, `Create`, `Edit`, and `Delete`.

Modules include Inbox, Tasks, Sprints, Projects, My Board, Attendance, Leave, Analytics, Files, Env Storage, Team Members, Holidays, and Shifts. Temporarily unavailable modules are hidden.

## Resolution rules

1. Admins retain full organization access.
2. A role defines the maximum operations the member can receive.
3. An override can enable or disable an operation within that ceiling.
4. An unset operation inherits the role default.

Managers can edit permissions for Members, but cannot grant an operation their target role is not allowed to hold. The server enforces the same result as the interface.

Use **Reset to role defaults** to remove all custom choices for a member.

## Change an override

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the member">
    Go to **Team Members**, select the person, then open the permission panel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the role baseline">
    The panel shows which operations are inherited from the role and which are available within its ceiling.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set only the exception">
    Enable or disable the necessary operation. Leave unrelated operations inherited so later role changes remain understandable.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save and verify">
    The change applies to navigation and server-side authorization. Ask the member to refresh if they already have the app open.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Recommended use

Use overrides to give a specific Member view-only access to a module, permit an eligible person to create a particular kind of work, or remove an operation from a Manager who should not perform it. Do not use overrides to model a department hierarchy or Project membership—those are separate controls.

<Warning>
Overrides do not bypass plan limits, organization membership, Project visibility, or a role ceiling. A saved-looking client change cannot grant access the server will reject.
</Warning>
