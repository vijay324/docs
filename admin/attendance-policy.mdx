title: "Attendance policy"
description: "Configure working hours, weekend rules, late arrivals, overtime, and past-date entries."
---

Attendance policy lives at **Settings → Attendance** and applies to every member of your organization.

[IMAGE: Attendance policy editor with working hours and rules]

## What you can configure

| Setting | What it does |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Standard working hours** | The expected check-in / check-out window. Used for late-arrival flags. |
| **Working days** | Monday through Friday by default; configurable per region. |
| **Weekend policy** | Whether weekend check-ins are allowed and how they appear in reports. |
| **Late-arrival window** | Threshold beyond which a check-in is flagged as late. |
| **Overtime threshold** | Hours per day above which overtime is flagged (default 8). Drives 1.5× OT in payroll. |
| **Allow past-date entries** | Whether members can backfill previous days, and how far back. |
| **Past-date limit** | The number of days back a member can backfill. Beyond that, only Admins can. |

## Late arrival

Set a window like `09:00–10:00`. Check-ins after 10:00 get the **late** flag, surfaced in the table view and the team overview.

## Overtime

If a check-out duration exceeds the overtime threshold, the day is flagged for OT. Flotick computes the hourly rate from the member's monthly gross and standard working days; OT contributes to payroll at 1.5×.

## Weekend behavior

Pick:

- **Block** — weekend check-ins are not allowed
- **Allow** — recorded normally
- **Allow but flag** — recorded with a weekend tag for reporting

## Backfill rules

Members may genuinely forget to check in or out. Configure how forgiving you want the system to be:

- `allowPastDates` on or off
- `pastDaysLimit` for how many days back self-backfill is allowed
- Beyond the limit, an Admin or Manager uses the **Forgotten attendance** dialog to backfill on the member's behalf

## Apply changes

Policy changes apply going forward. Past records are not re-evaluated.

## Tips

- Be reasonable on **late arrival**. Punitive flags reduce honest data over time.
- Use the **OT threshold** carefully. 8h is a common default but doesn't fit every team.
- Document your policy in the team handbook in addition to setting it in Flotick — clarity reduces friction.
