---
title: "Plan changes and retained data"
description: "How Flotick applies lower limits without deleting organization data."
---

Plan enforcement is non-destructive. A downgrade normally opens a seven-day grace window; the exact window can vary by deployment configuration.

## During grace

- The current higher-plan access remains active.
- The app shows the pending plan and deadline.
- Admins can reduce members and active Projects or restore the higher plan.

## After enforcement

- New actions above the lower limits are blocked.
- Excess newest non-Admin members are locked from signing in.
- Excess newest active Projects become read-only.
- Paid modules and advanced actions are hidden or blocked.
- Stored records remain in the organization.

The Admin is not plan-locked. Completed Projects do not count as active Projects.

## Restore access

Upgrade or reduce usage until it fits the plan. Flotick unlocks eligible members and Projects when capacity returns; retained work does not need to be recreated.

Before enforcement, remove or deactivate members who no longer need access, complete Projects that are finished, and remove unused files when storage exceeds the next allowance. Do not permanently delete work solely to avoid a reversible plan lock.

The billing workspace shows the pending plan, grace deadline, and restricted resources so the Admin can decide how to restore capacity.
