## Account protection

- Strong-password validation and secure password hashing
- Password reset by time-limited email token
- Temporary lockout after repeated failed sign-ins
- TOTP two-factor authentication for Admins and Managers
- Email verification for unfamiliar devices in protected sign-in flows
- Google account linking without creating a duplicate member
- Session refresh and sign-out controls

## Organization access

- Every signed-in request is scoped to an organization
- Role-based permissions for Admin, Manager, and Member
- Project membership and visibility checks
- Paid-plan per-member permission overrides
- Server-side validation even when an action is hidden in the interface

## Data protection

- HTTPS in transit in production
- Encrypted sensitive fields, including supported Task content and environment secrets
- Short-lived upload, view, and download access for files
- Soft-delete recovery for major records
- Rate limits on authentication, AI, upload, and high-volume actions

For account-specific controls, see [Account security](/account/security-and-2fa). To report a security concern, email [support@flotick.com](mailto:support@flotick.com) with **Security** in the subject.

## Your responsibilities

- Use a unique password and do not share it.
- Enable two-factor authentication when it is available to your role.
- Verify the organization slug and destination before adding files or secrets.
- Use Env Storage for environment values instead of comments or attachments.
- Review invitations, role changes, and plan changes carefully.
- Sign out of shared devices and avoid trusting them for future verification.

## What to report

Report suspected unauthorized access, a lost device with an active session, an exposed secret, an invitation sent to the wrong person, or unexpected changes to account or organization access. Include the organization slug, affected account, time, and a safe description of the issue. Do not include the secret or verification code itself.

## Security limitations

No service can eliminate all risk. Client-side controls, role restrictions, encryption, and temporary access URLs reduce exposure, but your organization remains responsible for choosing appropriate members, access levels, and data-handling practices.
